## Limitations & Uncertainty (Now Disclosed)

### Outcome Model Limitations
1. **Low R² (0.407)**: Model explains only 41% of variance
   - 59% of variance is unexplained
   - Other factors (surgeon skill, patient compliance, etc.) not captured

2. **High RMSE (14.63 points)**: Predictions have substantial uncertainty
   - Typical error: ±15 WOMAC points
   - A prediction of 26.7 points could realistically be 11.7-41.7 points

3. **Small Sample Size**: 379 patients is below ideal for robust modeling
   - Rule of thumb: Need 10×p = 200 patients (we have 379, so adequate but not large)
   - Limited generalizability to different populations

4. **Training Data Limitations**:
   - OAI data from 2004-2014 (temporal validation needed)
   - US-based population (geographic validation needed)
   - May not reflect current surgical techniques or patient populations

### Success Probability Calculation
- Based on improvement thresholds and historical outcomes
- **60% success probability** means: Based on similar patients in training data, 60% achieved ≥30 point improvement
- This is a **probability**, not a guarantee
